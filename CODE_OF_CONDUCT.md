# Code of Conduct

Helm is a small, practical open source project. The standard here is simple:
be direct, generous with context, and respectful of other contributors' time
and boundaries.

## Expected Behavior

- Assume good intent while staying precise about bugs and risks.
- Keep technical disagreement focused on the work.
- Avoid personal attacks, harassment, insults, or unwanted attention.
- Do not post someone else's private information, transcripts, screenshots,
  tokens, hostnames, or proprietary code.
- Respect maintainer decisions about scope, moderation, and release timing.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discriminatory language,
sexualized attention, repeated derailing, publishing private information, or
continuing a disruptive pattern after a maintainer asks you to stop.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or delete issues, pull requests, comments, or
discussions that violate this code. Maintainers may also temporarily or
permanently restrict participation when needed to keep the project usable for
contributors.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact a maintainer privately when a
private channel is available. For security-sensitive reports, follow
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
